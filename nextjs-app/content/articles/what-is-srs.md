# What is Spaced Repetition?

Spaced Repetition (SRS) is a scientifically-proven learning technique that helps you retain information more effectively by reviewing it at gradually increasing intervals. This method is based on the "spacing effect," a cognitive phenomenon where information is better remembered when it's reviewed over spaced intervals rather than crammed in a single session.

## How Does Spaced Repetition Work?

1. **Initial Learning**: You first learn new information
2. **First Review**: Review shortly after learning (e.g., 1 day)
3. **Increasing Intervals**: Each subsequent review happens after a longer period
4. **Adaptive Timing**: The interval increases when you remember well, decreases when you forget

## Benefits of Spaced Repetition

- **Long-term Retention**: Information stays in your memory longer
- **Efficient Learning**: Less time spent reviewing compared to traditional methods
- **Reduced Forgetting**: Minimizes the "forgetting curve"
- **Personalized Learning**: Adapts to your individual learning pace

## Common Spaced Repetition Algorithms

### SuperMemo 2
- One of the first computerized SRS algorithms
- Uses a simple formula to calculate intervals
- Basis for many modern SRS systems

### Anki's Algorithm
- Based on SuperMemo 2
- Adds "ease factor" to adjust intervals
- Includes leech detection for difficult cards

## Tips for Effective Spaced Repetition

1. **Be Consistent**: Regular reviews are key
2. **Quality Cards**: Create clear, concise cards
3. **Honest Ratings**: Rate your performance accurately
4. **Start Small**: Begin with a manageable number of cards
5. **Review Daily**: Maintain a daily review habit

## Getting Started

To start using spaced repetition:

1. Choose an SRS tool (like Anki or Memoria)
2. Create your first set of cards
3. Set a daily review goal
4. Stick to your review schedule
5. Monitor your progress

Remember, spaced repetition is a marathon, not a sprint. The benefits become more apparent over time as you build a strong foundation of knowledge.
